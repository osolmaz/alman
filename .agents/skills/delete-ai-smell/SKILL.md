# Delete AI smell

AI-written prose has recognizable tells. Readers pattern-match on them and
discount the text. Apply these rules to all prose written for this project,
and sweep for violations before finishing any writing task.

## Banned patterns

- **Em dashes.** At most one set per 1000 words. Restructure with commas,
  parentheses, or separate sentences instead.
- **Colon punchiness.** Do not compensate for the em-dash rule with "X: Y"
  constructions ("Alman is our answer: a simplified..."). Colons are for
  genuine lists only. Semicolons should be rare.
- **Contrast rhetoric.** "It is not X, it is Y", "X, not Y", "not X, but Y"
  and all variants ("changes the packaging, not the position") are banned.
  Say what the thing is in a plain sentence.
- **"Not just X" escalation.** "It isn't just X, it's Y" and similar
  intensifier patterns are banned for the same reason.

## Other tells to avoid

- Rule-of-three everywhere. Lists of exactly three parallel items in
  sentence after sentence are a strong tell. Vary list length, or use
  fewer lists.
- Overwrought transition words ("moreover", "furthermore", "in conclusion")
  and summary paragraphs that restate what was just said.
- Inflated vocabulary where a plain word exists ("delve", "landscape",
  "testament to", "tapestry", "crucial", "leverage" as a verb).
- Symmetric sentence rhythm. If every sentence has the same shape and
  length, break it up.
- Hedging boilerplate ("it's worth noting that", "it's important to
  remember").

## How to write instead

Write plain sentences. Lead with the point. Prefer concrete nouns and
numbers over abstractions. Read the paragraph aloud in your head; if it
sounds like a keynote or a LinkedIn post, rewrite it flatter. When editing
existing text, fix the smell by restructuring the sentence, and do not
merely swap one banned punctuation mark or pattern for another.
